Deep Learning

Deep Learning methods, generally associated with Neural Networks, are rising a great deal of interest. From our point of view, three major reasons cause this interest:

  1. Great performance: Deep Neural Networks have obtained outstanding performances in computer vision, natural language processing, and speech recognition.
  2. Parallelism with human brain: Deep Neural Networks have also raised interest and debate int the neuroscience world. The debate in this case is mostly centered around whether these networks are an appropriate model for human brains, and to what extent standard training methods such as stochastic gradient descent can be useful to reproduce the complex thought processes involved in human thinking.
  3. The more data, the better:
  4. From raw data to labels in one step: let is consider the case of computer vision, these learning methods ingest images pixel by pixel, and apply a series of nonlinear transformations that allow higher level information to be learned. This way, the network itself extract the features relevant from the task at hand. By looking at the outputs of individual neurons in the network, it has been shown that they are able to identify find not only edges, but also complex shapes like faces.

We are particularly interested in the last point. Automating or even skipping the feature extraction process can have a huge impact in many domains.

While neural networks will, roughly speaking, do the job in almost any machine learning problem, it is worth determining when they are the right tool to use. There are two main reasons that can make their use inappropriate. The first is that large networks might just be an overkill, presenting a great training overhead. The second case is readability and interpretability. In many domains, from medicine to economy, the prediction itself can be almost as important as the reasoning that lead to make it. In this sense, neural networks, while they are not black boxes, they are highly complex models that are hardly interpretable by humans.
